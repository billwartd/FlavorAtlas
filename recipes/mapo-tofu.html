Mapo Tofu

There are dishes that feed you, and then there are dishes that teach you something about a culture. Mapo Tofu is firmly in the second camp. Originating from China, this recipe has been refined over centuries, with each generation adding their own subtle touches while respecting the fundamental principles that make it work. Sichuan's iconic tofu with fiery numbing chili-bean sauce and minced pork.

Ingredients

  • 500g protein of choice
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic minced
  • 1 inch ginger minced
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp chili paste or flakes
  • 2 green onions sliced
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, Thai basil, or mint)
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare all your ingredients before you start cooking. This is the most important step in any recipe — mis en place is not optional. Chop, measure, and organize everything within arm's reach.
  2. Season your protein generously with salt and the appropriate spices for this dish. Let it come to room temperature for 15-20 minutes before cooking — cold protein cooks unevenly.
  3. Heat your cooking vessel until it is properly hot. Add oil, then add the protein. Do not move it for the first 2-3 minutes — let it develop a proper sear. Flip once and cook the other side.
  4. Remove the protein and set aside. In the same vessel, cook the aromatics — onion, garlic, ginger, or whatever the recipe calls for. These build the flavor foundation.
  5. Add the liquid component — stock, tomatoes, coconut milk, or cream. Scrape up any browned bits from the bottom. This is where the flavor lives.
  6. Return the protein to the liquid. Simmer gently until cooked through. The sauce should reduce and concentrate in flavor.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning. Add acid (lime, lemon, vinegar) if the dish tastes flat. Add salt if it lacks depth. Add heat if it needs excitement.
  8. Serve immediately with the appropriate accompaniment. Garnish generously. Eat while hot — most dishes do not improve with waiting.

💡 Pro Tip

Use the best ingredients you can find. This dish has few components, which means each one needs to pull its weight. A mediocre ingredient cannot hide in a simple recipe.
